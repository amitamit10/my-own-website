## Summary

- **PASS (clean, usable as display):** H, S, V, X, 3 (5 glyphs)
- **NEEDS WORK (manually fixable in an image editor):** W, 4, 5, 6 (4 glyphs)
- **NEEDS WORK (re-paint or re-photograph required):** A, B, C, D, E, F, G, I, J, K, L, M, N, O, P, Q, R, T, U, Y, Z, 0, 1, 2, 7, 8, 9 (27 glyphs)

**Common failure patterns:**
1. **Background bleed-through (dark halos):** U, Y, Z, 7, 9 have large dark smudges; 4, 5, 6 have smaller halos. rembg appears to have under-masked these letters, leaving the original dark surface in the alpha channel.
2. **White foam/cotton cores:** O, 0, Q, 2, 8 — the original photograph included white cotton balls or foam that was not removed, leaving a white block in the middle of round letters.
3. **Missing structural strokes:** A (no crossbar), B (no spine), E (no middle arm), F (no middle arm), G (no spur), I (no serifs), J (no top bar), K (no upper arm), M (no inner V), N (no right leg), R (no leg).
4. **Melted / distorted tops:** D, L, 1.
5. **Closed counters (should be open):** C, G.
6. **Stray spatters:** I, W, 5, 8.
